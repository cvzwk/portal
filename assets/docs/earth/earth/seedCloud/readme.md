2. **Accretion:** As the supercooled water droplets in the cloud come into contact with the silver iodide particles, they freeze onto the particles, gradually building up layers of ice. This process is known as accretion.

3. **Collision and Coalescence:** As the ice crystals continue to grow, they may collide with other ice crystals or supercooled water droplets present in the cloud. When collisions occur, smaller ice crystals can stick together, forming larger and more complex ice crystals.

4. **Aggregation:** Over time, as the ice crystals collide and aggregate, they become larger and more massive. This process of ice crystals combining through collisions is known as aggregation.

5. **Falling as Precipitation:** As the ice crystals grow larger and heavier, they eventually become too heavy to be suspended in the cloud's updrafts. At this point, they fall from the cloud as precipitation, which can manifest as snow or rain depending on the temperature.
